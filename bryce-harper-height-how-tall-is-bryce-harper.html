    Bryce Harper height-American baseball designated hitter, Bryce Aron Max Harper was born on October 16th, 1992 in Las Vegas, Nevada.

    In Las Vegas, Nevada, Harper attended Las Vegas High School. Sports Illustrated compared Harper to LeBron James by referring to him as his sport’s “Chosen One” in a cover article in May 2009.

    He reclassified and became eligible earlier for the Major League Baseball (MLB) draft in June 2010 after receiving his GED in October 2009, during his junior year.

    Harper, then 17 years old, signed up to play catcher for the College of Southern Nevada in the National Junior College Athletic Association (NJCAA)’s Scenic West Athletic Conference (SWAC) in 2010.

    One of the starting pitchers for the Southern Nevada Coyotes, his older brother Bryan, who had been their teammate in high school, worked frequently as a battery for him.

    The SWAC employs wooden bats in conference play, just like MLB, which proved advantageous for Harper as he eventually made the transition to an MLB career.

    He had 31 home runs and 98 RBI in 66 games while batting.443,.526 on-base percentage, and.987 slugging percentage. Harper was given the 2010 SWAC Player of the Year after setting a new school record in 2010 with 31 home runs, breaking the previous mark of 12.

    Harper went 6-for-7 with five RBIs and a cycle in the Western district finals of the 2010 NJCAA World Series.

    He went 2-for-5 with a three-run double in the first game of a doubleheader the following day. He had a 6-for-6 performance with four home runs, a triple, and a double in the second game.

    Home plate umpire Don Gilmore dismissed Harper from a National Junior College World Series game on June 2 of that year for questioning a called third strike.

    As he departed the plate, Harper evidently wanted to indicate where he thought the pitch was by drawing a line in the ground with his bat. Harper received a two-game ban as a result of his second ejection of the season.

    Southern Nevada lost the game in which Harper was disqualified, and the ban put an end to his amateur career. The group lost their subsequent game while Harper was out of the lineup, eliminating them from the competition.

    The Golden Spikes Award, given annually to the finest amateur baseball player in the country, went to Harper in 2010.

    Bryce Harper career

    Bryce Harper is one if the most famous athletes in baseball and right fielder for the Major League Baseball (MLB) Philadelphia Phillies. Harper played with the Washington Nationals from 2012 to 2018 before moving to Philadelphia.

    Harper, one of the most highly anticipated draft picks in recent memory, has been called a “five-tool player.” He transferred to the College of Southern Nevada after his sophomore year of Las Vegas High School, where he received the 2010 Golden Spikes Award.

    In the 2010 MLB Draft, the Nationals picked Harper with the first overall pick. On April 28, 2012, at the age of 19, he made his MLB debut with the Nationals.

    Being the youngest position player to participate in an All-Star Game, Harper was chosen for the 2012 All-Star Game.

    Harper shared the NL lead in home runs in 2015 and won the National League (NL) Rookie of the Year Award in 2012.

    He was selected as the National League Most Valuable Player for 2015 by the Baseball Writers’ Association of America, and at the age of 22, he became the youngest MLB player to receive the honor.

    He signed a 13-year, $330 million deal with the Phillies as a free agency during the 2018–19 offseason, which at the time was the most expensive deal ever in North American sports before being surpassed shortly after by Mike Trout.

    With the Phillies, he earned the NL MVP award for the second time in 2021. The Phillies won their first pennant since 2009 thanks in large part to his leadership the following season, and he was named the NLCS MVP in the process. This was the team’s first postseason participation in 11 years.
